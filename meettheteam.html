Meet the Cyclone Cowboys!


Coming from Oklahoma, it makes sense that Oklahoma State University has interest in alternative energy. Wind energy accounts for about 44% of our state's total energy output. Our team is made up of a diverse group of people, from 23.81% women, to electrical, industrial, mechanical, aerospace, civil, and environmental majors. We also recruit all college years. Together, from out Turbine Design, Project Development, and Outreach team, we address the Department of Energy's Collegiate Wind Competition. It is our first year and we have made tons of progress!




Project Development

Project Development is involved in creating a hypothetical offshore wind farm. They have to take into account cost, marine and avian wildlife, along with the ecosystem. They were assigned a plot of sea in the Coast of Oregon to work with. In the plot, and oil pipe runs by it so figuring out anchor designs and placements is challenging.


Turbine Design

Turbine Design is the brains of our team. They design, fabricate, and manufacture the wind turbine that is to be used at contest. from creating the Nacelle, to choosing an appropriate motor, to 3D printing the blades, they have created and got over the challenges that come from building a wind turbine.


Outreach Team

Outreach Team keeps out club and the Senior Design project strong! From recruiting new members, especially underclassmen, to going to local elementary schools to teach about wind energy, they have done a lot of educate the community.
